Densha Otaku

こんにちは! Welcome to Densha Otaku, the definitive Japanese train simulator archive and encyclopaedia.

Please note: This site is currently under construction

What is this project?

Densha Otaku (literally: 'Train Geek' in Japanese) is a repository of information about Japanese train simulators. The team behind the site also operates a professional archival service for software, images, and supporting literature relating to them.

Getting started

Not sure where to start? You'll find plenty of resources over on the left navigation menu. If you're looking for simulators and games check out the 'Software' section, similarly if you're looking for trains and vehicles featured in simulator titles, look under 'Rolling Stock'.

Who runs the project?

The site and the related archival project is run by a small team of dedicated volunteers based in the United Kingdom. We do it because we love what we do.

How can I help?

It costs a lot to run a preservation project. Be that time, effort, and financial expense - but there's a number of ways you can contribute:

  • If you click this #missing tag, it'll show others across the site, it means we're looking for items that are missing from the archive. This could be a physical item, high-resolution scan of a document, or many other things.
  • If you click an #incomplete tag, it'll show you instances where the information we hold is incomplete or lacking in detail.

If you'd like to donate to the project, we'll be setting up ways for the community to donate knowledge or the archive in the near future once we're all set up. We appreciate your patience.

Why is this site in English?

The volunteers that run this project all speak English as their primary language, with Japanese as their secondary. The intent is that we translate the entire site into Japanese so it can be fully utilised by those in the country of origin.
